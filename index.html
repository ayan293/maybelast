Poem 1 | Her Silence Owns Me

Your silence is not absence— it’s a blade pressed against my chest. Every time you fall quiet, I feel chained to the weight of you, like even your unspoken thoughts have the power to undo me. Others talk, they shout, but your silence is the only sound that has ever broken me beautifully.

Poem 2 | The Fire That Burns Me Whole

You are not gentle. And that is why I cannot leave. You are storm and ruin, and I crave the wreckage. Every word from you scars me deeper, every glance makes the rest of the world dissolve. I don’t want their softness— I want the violence of your fire, because only in your flames do I feel alive enough to bleed.

Poem 3 | The Possession I Chose

I don’t just love you— I surrendered to you. Every breath I take tastes of your name, every night is a grave I lie in, waiting for the echo of you. Call it obsession, call it madness, but understand this: No illusion, no shadow, no dream man will ever love you the way I already do. I am the wound you never asked for, and the devotion that will haunt you forever.

My Love Letter

My Eternal Flame,

One year ago your confession shattered the silence I was trapped in. In that moment my world was rewritten and my soul was claimed by you forever.

They call love light and gentle, but ours was never meant for softness. Ours is carved in shadows | sealed in silence | and bound by a devotion too fierce for anyone else to understand.

You are the calm that silences my storms | the ache that keeps me alive | the answer to every question I never dared to ask. Every heartbeat I own belongs to you, willingly chained and beautifully surrendered.

I carry your name like a scar that refuses to fade. If eternity gave me the choice again, I would fall to you once more even if it meant burning until the end of time.

Eternally Yours,

A full year has passed, my love. Twelve months where I burned quietly while you dreamed of men who exist only in shadows and screens.

You called me the real one, and yet your heart wandered toward illusions— figures who never bleed, never break, never feel the weight of silence the way I do.

I bleed. I break. I am not flawless. And maybe that was my only crime: being too real to ever be your dream.

I loved you in ways they never could. Not even in the worlds you escape to. My love was flawed, fragile, scarred— but it was real. Their words are perfect, their faces untouchable, yet your silence cut me deeper than all their promises combined.

What they gave you was safe and hollow, a dream that could never bleed. What I gave you was myself— a body that breaks, a soul that bends, devotion that burned even when it was unseen.

And maybe that is why you never felt it. Because I was never extraordinary, never a dream worth chasing. I was only the nothingness beneath your fantasies, the quiet proof that someone loved you harder than fiction ever dared to.

Maybe I am just the void, the silence between your fantasies, the man who loves you deeply but will never shine like the illusions you chase.
